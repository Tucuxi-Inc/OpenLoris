## User Types

Loris has two primary user types with distinct roles and permissions:

| User Type | Also Called | Primary Purpose |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Business User** | Requester, Asker | Ask questions, receive answers |
| **Domain Expert** | Expert, Owner, SME | Answer questions, manage knowledge |

*Note: A user can hold both roles (e.g., a senior attorney who asks questions of colleagues in other practice areas while also being an expert in their own area).*

---

## Persona 1: The Business User (Question Asker)

### "Alex" - Marketing Manager

**Demographics:**
- Mid-level manager, 5 years at company
- Non-legal background
- Needs legal guidance occasionally (2-3x per month)

**Goals:**
- Get quick, reliable answers to legal questions
- Not have to remember who to email or Slack
- Track the status of pending questions
- Reference previous answers easily

**Pain Points:**
- "I never know if my email to Legal got read"
- "I asked the same question 6 months ago but can't find the answer"
- "I don't know how to phrase legal questions properly"
- "Sometimes I wait a week and have to follow up"

**Comfort Level:**
- Comfortable with web applications
- Not technical, prefers simple interfaces
- Values clear status indicators

## User Quotes (Design Principles)

These quotes should guide design decisions:

### Business Users Want:
> "Just tell me if anyone's working on my question."

> "I don't need to know how the sausage is made - just give me the answer."

> "If Legal already answered this before, why do I have to wait?"

> "I want to help myself when I can, but know when I need an expert."

### Domain Experts Want:
> "Show me what we already know before I start researching."

> "Don't make me answer the same question twice."

> "I want control over what gets automated."

> "Help me see if my answers are actually being used."

### Administrators Want:
> "I need data to justify our value to the business."

> "Show me where we're spending the most time."

> "Help me identify training opportunities."

> "I want to see trends, not just snapshots."
